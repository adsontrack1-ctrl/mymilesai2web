New Features: Bulk Trip Classification, CRA Support, and CSV Export

May 2026  ·  5 min read

This release focuses on three things our users have been asking for since launch: a way to classify a week's worth of trips at once, proper support for Canadian users filing under CRA rules, and a CSV export format that works directly with accounting software like QuickBooks, Wave, and FreshBooks.

Here's everything that changed and how to use it.

⚡

Bulk Classify

Select multiple trips and mark them all as Business, Personal, or Medical in one tap.

🍁

CRA CSV Export

Canadian users can now export a logbook formatted for T2125 and T777 filing with per-km totals.

🤖

Smarter AI

Improved classification accuracy for repeat destinations — the AI now recognizes your regular client locations.

Bulk Trip Classification

Previously, every trip had to be reviewed and classified individually. For users who drive frequently for work — realtors doing 8–10 showings a day, rideshare drivers, delivery contractors — this meant a long review queue at the end of the week.

Bulk classify works like this:

  1. Open the Trips tab and tap Select in the top right corner.
  2. Tap trips to select them. You can also tap the date header to select all trips on that day.
  3. With multiple trips selected, choose Business, Personal, or Medical from the action bar at the bottom of the screen.
  4. Done. All selected trips are classified instantly.

Bulk classify is available on iOS and Android. The web app dashboard supports the same workflow on desktop — useful for year-end cleanup or reviewing a long trip history when you first set up the app.

A Note on AI Pre-Classification

We've also updated our AI classification engine. It now looks at your last 60 days of classification behavior to identify patterns — for example, if you always mark trips to a specific address as "Business — Client Visit," the AI will automatically suggest the same classification for future trips to that address.

This doesn't replace the bulk tool — it's a complement to it. Destinations you visit regularly will be pre-classified by the AI. One-off or ambiguous trips can be handled with bulk selection. Together, most users can stay fully caught up with under two minutes of weekly review.

CRA-Formatted CSV Export

Canadian users have had access to standard PDF exports since launch, but our CSV export previously used mile-based calculations and US field names — not useful for Canadian tax filing, which requires kilometres and Canadian terminology.

The new CRA CSV export mode activates automatically when you set your locale to Canada in app settings. It generates a spreadsheet with the following columns:

CRA CSV — Column Reference
DateTrip date (YYYY-MM-DD)
Start AddressOrigin address or "Home" if location is your registered home
End AddressDestination address or business name
KilometresDistance in km (converted from GPS data)
PurposeBusiness purpose (from your trip note or AI suggestion)
CategoryBusiness / Personal / Medical
Allowance ($)CRA prescribed rate calculation: $0.73/km (first 5,000) or $0.67/km (after)
Running KM TotalCumulative business km for the year (to track the 5,000 km threshold)

The Allowance column calculates the dollar value of each trip at the current CRA prescribed rates and automatically switches from $0.73/km to $0.67/km once your annual business kilometres exceed 5,000. You don't need to track this threshold manually.

At year-end, your total from the Allowance column is the figure you enter as your motor vehicle expense on Form T2125 (self-employed) or T777 (employees). Export, open in Excel or Numbers, and your logbook is complete.

Note for employees (T777 filers): The CRA export satisfies the logbook requirement if your employer signs a T2200 and you are not reimbursed at the prescribed rate. The export also works if you're claiming actual expenses — use it to calculate your business-use percentage rather than the per-kilometre allowance column.
